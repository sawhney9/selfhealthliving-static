Set aside.</p></li><li><p><strong>Cook the Eggs: </strong>Heat olive oil or avocado oil in a skillet over medium heat. <p><span style="font-weight: 400">This is one of my favorites for a few reasons, first most it tastes amazing, and secondly it is so simple and quick to put together. It is a breakfast meal my kids love and it’s usually quicker than whipping together pancakes and so much healthier. This option is gluten free and you can easily be dairy free. You are combining some protein and choline from the eggs and the prosciutto, as well as a good source of vitamins b2, b3, b6, b12 and zinc. One of the other reasons I love this is because prosciutto is so flavorful that you don’t need a lot to add a pop to your meal. One slice typically has about 4 grams of protein and about 1.75 grams of unsaturated fat. </span></p>
<p><b>Concerns:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Yes it is true that the World health Organization (WHO) has classified all processed meat as a carcinogen and they have been linked to cancer in some studies. For that reason we don’t advocate making prosciutto or any processed meat as part of your everyday diet, but occasionally it can be enjoyed as a treat. For anyone that visits Italy or Spain you will notice Prosciutto and ham are regularly consumed, yet the </span><a href="https://www.wcrf.org/preventing-cancer/cancer-statistics/colorectal-cancer-statistics/"><span style="font-weight: 400">colon cancer rates</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> are significantly lower than other countries. Prosciutto is still considered one of the healthier of the processed meats, as it tends to have less saturated fat than other processed meats. Take </span><span style="font-weight: 400">prosciutto's 1.5 grams of saturated fat per ounce compared to pan-fried</span><a href="https://tools.myfooddata.com/nutrition-facts.php?food=168322&amp;serv=oz&amp;qty=1"><span style="font-weight: 400"> bacon</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> (3.4 grams) or </span><a href="https://tools.myfooddata.com/nutrition-facts/174587/wt2/1"><span style="font-weight: 400">pork sausage</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> (2.2 grams). If you decide this is not a risk you want to take you can always just remove it altogether or find a low fat substitute such as turkey.</span></p>

<h3><b>Recipe Notes</b></h3>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Tortilla Options</b><span style="font-weight: 400">: </span><span style="font-weight: 400">I am a huge fan of the </span><a href="https://amzn.to/3ZiEFFN"><span style="font-weight: 400">Siete Almond tortillas</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">, which are regularly available in just about any grocery store these days, but you can also use cassava or regular tortilla.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Egg Cooking Method</b><span style="font-weight: 400">: Adjust the cooking method of the eggs based on your preference. Scrambled eggs are my go to mainly because it's so quick, while fried eggs have a crispy edge.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Avocado Ripeness</b><span style="font-weight: 400">: Choose a ripe avocado for easier mashing. It should yield slightly when pressed. If you don’t have time to mash the avocado, scooping out half and slicing it is a great alternative, just sprinkle a little salt and pepper to taste.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Seasoning Variations</b><span style="font-weight: 400">: Feel free to adapt the seasoning based on your taste. Add a pinch of chili flakes for heat or fresh herbs like spinach for extra flavor.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Meal Prep</b><span style="font-weight: 400">: The avocado can brown quickly; if preparing in advance, consider adding lemon juice to the mashed avocado to help preserve its color.</span></p>

One bowl delivers about 22g of plant protein and 15g of fiber — and the beets bring something most protein bowls don't: dietary nitrate that your body converts into nitric oxide for better blood flow.

This is the kind of meal that earns its place in a rotation. Black beluga lentils hold their shape, the roasted beets caramelize into something genuinely good, and the tahini-lemon sauce ties it together. It reheats well and costs almost nothing per serving.

## What Each Ingredient Is Doing

**Black beluga lentils** — The protein and fiber backbone. Lentils are one of the few foods that hit both high protein and high fiber without animal products, and legume intake is consistently tied to lower all-cause mortality in longitudinal cohorts. A pooled analysis across five cohorts found legumes were the single most protective food predictor of survival in older adults across cultures ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15228991/)). The black skin also carries anthocyanins — the same polyphenol class that makes blueberries dark.

**Beets** — The reason this bowl is more than a lentil dish. Beets are dense in inorganic nitrate, which the body reduces to nitric oxide, lowering blood pressure and improving endothelial function. A randomized trial showed beetroot juice dropped systolic blood pressure meaningfully in hypertensive adults ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25421976/)). Roasting concentrates the sweetness without destroying the nitrate.

**Walnuts** — The only common nut with a substantial dose of alpha-linolenic acid, a plant omega-3. In the PREDIMED trial, a nut-supplemented Mediterranean diet cut major cardiovascular events ([NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1800389)). They also add crunch and satiety fat.

**Tahini** — Sesame paste brings calcium, copper, and lignans. Sesame lignans have been shown to lower oxidative stress markers and blood pressure in controlled feeding studies ([PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16484570/)).

**Smoked paprika & cumin** — Not just flavor. Cumin and paprika carry carotenoids and have measurable antioxidant capacity, and the smoky depth means you need far less salt to make this taste complete.

## Nutrition Per Serving (approx.)

- **Protein:** 22g
- **Fiber:** 15g
- **Fat:** 24g (mostly from walnuts, tahini, olive oil)
- **Carbs:** 38g

## Meal Prep Notes

This bowl was built for batch cooking. Cook the lentils and roast the beets together on day one, store them in separate containers, and they hold for 5 days in the fridge.

Keep the tahini sauce in its own jar — it thickens cold, so loosen it with a splash of warm water before serving. Add the spinach fresh and the walnuts last to keep the texture.

Variations: swap beets for roasted carrots if you want lower sugar, or add a soft-boiled egg or crumbled feta if you eat dairy and want to push protein past 30g. For a grain version, serve over farro or barley — pairs well with our [high-fiber breakfast approach](/fuel/high-fiber-breakfast).

## The Honest Reality

Beets stain everything and roasting them takes 35 minutes — there's no way around that. And the nitrate benefit is real but modest; this is a heart-healthy meal, not a blood-pressure medication. The honest takeaway: build meals like this into a weekly rotation and the compounding adds up. One bowl won't change your biomarkers. Fifty of them, over a year, will.

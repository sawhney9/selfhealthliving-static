<p><b>Health Benefits:</b></p>
<ul>
<li><b>Strawberries:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Packed with vitamin C and antioxidants, good for immunity.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Sauerkraut:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Probiotic-rich, aids digestion and boosts gut health.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Carrots:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> High in beta-carotene, supporting eye and skin health.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Apple:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Provides fiber and antioxidants, promoting heart health.</span></li>
<li><b>Cauliflower:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Low-calorie, fiber-rich, and supports digestion.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Almond Butter:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Provides healthy fats and protein, good for satiety and heart health.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><b>Protein Powder:</b><span> Boosts muscle repair and overall recovery.</span></li>
<li><b>Flaxseed Powder:</b><span style="font-weight: 400"> Rich in omega-3s, supporting heart health and reducing inflammation.</span></li>
</ul>
<p>Let's dive a little more into each ingredient, highlighting the benefits and the studies that help support these benefits. </p>
<p><strong>1 Cup of Frozen Strawberries</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Strawberries are rich in antioxidants, particularly vitamin C and manganese. They help boost immune health, improve skin health, and reduce inflammation.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Consumption of strawberries is linked to improved heart health and reduced </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7828105/"><span style="font-weight: 400">oxidative stress</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400"><strong>¼ cup Sauerkraut</strong> (Yea you read that right)</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Benefits: Being fermented, sauerkraut is rich in probiotics, which are the beneficial bacteria that support gut health, digestion, and the immune system. A healthy gut can also positively impact mental health through the gut-brain connection.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">These probiotics help maintain a balanced gut flora, aiding in digestion and reducing symptoms like bloating or constipation. Sauerkraut is high in vitamins C and K, which support immune function and bone health.</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400"><span style="font-weight: 400">Studies: Regular consumption of sauerkraut has shown to</span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9606823/"><span style="font-weight: 400"> improve immune function</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and protect against inflammation with the introduction of possible bacteria in the gut biome.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><strong>4-5 Baby Carrots</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Carrots are high in beta-carotene, which the body converts to vitamin A, crucial for good vision, immune function, and skin health. They also provide dietary fiber and several other vitamins and minerals.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Regular consumption of carrots is associated with improved </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6770766/"><span style="font-weight: 400">eye health</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and reduced risk of </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6770766/"><span style="font-weight: 400">certain cancers</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">.</span></p>
<p><strong>⅓ of an Apple</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Apples are rich in fiber, particularly pectin, which aids in digestion and helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels. They are also a good source of vitamin C and various antioxidants.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Apples have been linked to lower risk of </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9321083/"><span style="font-weight: 400">chronic diseases</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">, including cardiovascular disease.</span></p>
<p><strong> 4-5 Florets of Cauliflower</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Cauliflower is </span><span style="font-weight: 400">such a great source of fiber and antioxidants. It is a great source of vitamin K that helps with calcium absorption for stronger bones. It's one of those superfoods that can fit into just about any diet plan, if you are keto, mediterranean or vegan.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">– Studies: </span><span style="font-weight: 400">Cauliflower is </span><span style="font-weight: 400">included in the Cruciferous family and thus the components of cauliflower include glucosinolates, ascorbic acid, carotenoids, phenolic compounds and vitamin E.</span> <span style="font-weight: 400">These vitamins and compounds give it its anticarcinogenic and antioxidant effects. Studies mostly have focused on the benefits of cauliflower and other cruciferous vegetables with lowering risk of cardiovascular, cancer and neurological disorders but new evidence is showing the </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9521401/"><span style="font-weight: 400">benefits in bone health</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> especially for perimenopausal women.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Nuts and nut butters have been linked to a </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3257681/"><span style="font-weight: 400">lower risk of cardiovascular disease</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and improved blood lipid profiles.</span></p>
<p><strong>1 Serving of Protein Powder</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Protein powder is an excellent source of high-quality protein, which is essential for muscle repair and growth. It helps keep you full and can aid in weight management.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: </span><a href="https://amzn.to/40unmE3"><span style="font-weight: 400">Protein</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> supplementation is beneficial for </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3905294/"><span style="font-weight: 400">muscle recovery</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and overall muscle health, especially post-exercise.</span></p>
<p><strong>1 Teaspoon of Fine Flaxseed Powder </strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Flaxseed oil is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which support heart health, reduce inflammation, and promote brain function. It also provides fiber that aids in digestion.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Flaxseed oil consumption is associated with improved cardiovascular health and </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10353157/"><span style="font-weight: 400">anti-inflammatory effects</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">.</span></p>

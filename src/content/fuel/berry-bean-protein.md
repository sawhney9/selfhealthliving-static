<p><b>Ingredients and Health Benefits:</b></p>
<p><strong>1 Cup of mixed berries</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400"> </span><span style="font-weight: 400">- Benefits: Mixed berries, such as blueberries, strawberries, and raspberries, are rich in antioxidants, particularly vitamin C and anthocyanins. These antioxidants help combat oxidative stress, reduce inflammation, and support overall immune health.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   - Studies: Research shows that regular consumption of berries is associated with improved heart health and cognitive function.</span></p>
<p><strong>2/3 Cup Frozen Spinach</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   - Benefits: Spinach is packed with vitamins A, C, and K, as well as folate, iron, and calcium. It supports eye health, boosts immune function, and helps maintain healthy blood pressure levels.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   - Studies: Spinach's high antioxidant content provides cardiovascular benefits and reduces oxidative stress  .</span></p>
<p><strong>⅓ of an Apple</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Apples are rich in fiber, particularly pectin, which aids in digestion and helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels. They are also a good source of vitamin C and various antioxidants.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Apples have been linked to lower risk of </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9321083/"><span style="font-weight: 400">chronic diseases</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">, including cardiovascular disease.</span></p>
<p><strong>½ cup of Greek Yogurt</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   - Benefits: Greek yogurt is a great source of protein, probiotics, calcium, and vitamin B12. It supports gut health by promoting a healthy balance of gut bacteria and aids in digestion.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   - Studies: Probiotics in Greek yogurt have been shown to improve digestive health and enhance immune function  .</span></p>

It supports heart health, helps control blood sugar levels, and promotes satiety.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Nuts and nut butters have been linked to a </span><a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3257681/"><span style="font-weight: 400">lower risk of cardiovascular disease</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and improved blood lipid profiles.</span></p>
<p><strong>¼ cup Garbanzo beans</strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">–Benefits: Garbanzo beans are sometimes known as the super bean because of all the benefits they provide. They are a great source of fiber, protein, antioxidants and a good source of iron. Not only has it been shown to be good for your gut and heart but also is a top contender of food eaten within the </span><a href="https://www.bluezones.com/"><span style="font-weight: 400">Blue Zones</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">– Studies: Garbanzo beans have been shown to </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10580981/"><span style="font-weight: 400">lower diabetes</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> and help women specifically with </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8067853/"><span style="font-weight: 400">low iron levels</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400"> especially for those that tend to be vegan or vegetation.</span></p>
<p><strong>1 Serving of Protein Powder</strong></p>

<p><strong>1 Teaspoon of Fine Flaxseed Powder </strong></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Benefits: Flaxseed oil is rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which support heart health, reduce inflammation, and promote brain function. It also provides fiber that aids in digestion.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400">   – Studies: Flaxseed oil consumption is associated with improved cardiovascular health and </span><a href="https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10353157/"><span style="font-weight: 400">anti-inflammatory effects</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400">.</span></p>
